My experience with the development of my website:

Although I am still in my brainstorming phase, I have realized that with the knowledge I have attained, I need to conceptualize my idea in a more realistic manner. I have been intrigued by bike website layouts (Ducati, Honda, Kawasaki and the classic ones such as Harley Davidson and Indian). I have gathered that each of them has a “perk” somewhere in their website that makes it unique. Indian, for example, has a 3D model of the selected bike and shows what the bike would look like with certain add-ons for example another exhaust. I would like to achieve a special and unique perk for my website that ender users would appreciate. Although my progress has been moving slowly, I have more clarity with my end goal.

Reflection of the reading and lecture and how they tie into UI / UX:

UI and UX tie into each other so that the end user is comfortable while using the app or website. The key aspects that I have gathered from the reading is that interactivity is the foundation of UI/ UX designs. Interactivity is about responsive behavior, does not have complete control and is not random. If the user can interact successfully then YAYY but if not, then it's back to the drawing board until we get a YAYY WE DID IT!

Perspective of course material:

From the previous learning material, I learnt that the use of specificity helps the user pick out important words, phrases or actions in a website without overriding the headings importance. If the same specificity is used in the heading and the text, it will clash and become overwhelming for the end user. In the second section of the course material, the definition of interaction is emphasized as mentioned in the reflection of the readings and lectures. One factor that was highlighted by Ted Nelson was that the “origins of content” are interconnected and represented in hypermedia. This stands out because without the first steps of research, would the internet exist? Would the non -trivial action of clicking and typing ever replace the traditional action of flipping a page? Would journals and academic readings be available at our fingertips? In my opinion, traditional actions will always remain special as it activates 4 senses (touch, hear, see, smell). A non-trivial action could replace the sound of pages flipping and the visuals of a book through graphics and sound effects, but it would not be authentic. The origins of content have a special place and activate many memories long before the internet entered the chat. We can appreciate how far we've come however we should still value the old. There is still much to learn about interaction from older content, such as how did people achieve this when the internet did not exist. We can use these pointers to establish better UI/ UX systems for users.
