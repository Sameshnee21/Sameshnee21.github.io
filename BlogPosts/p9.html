Digital Inequalities and why they matter:

Digital inequality is an issue that cannot be easily reversed or rehabilitated overnight. It is a way of life and has a “distinct presence” in our society (Robinson et al: 2015). Digital inequality is when part of the population id digitally dependent whilst the other part is digitally disadvantaged. Basic use of technology is a must in today’s world and is a crucial part of life as the world is going digital whether or not people appreciate it.

Reference:

Robinson et al. 2015. Information, Communication and Society: Digital Inequalities and Why They Matter.
