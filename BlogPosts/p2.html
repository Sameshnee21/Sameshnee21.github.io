My experience starting HTML:

My experience with HTML has been very exciting as the lecture video is informative and well put together for a beginner. Initially, I was nervous as last year's coding went south in many ways. I find that HTML is easier to understand and highly enjoyable. I look forward to the process of building my first website with the knowledge that I have attained thus far.

Reflection of readings:

In the reading, “Why you should choose HTML5”, I found that the passage was well detailed in terms of examples and actual code to refer to. As someone new to coding, I understood the text to an extent of the different between article and section. Before the reading, I thought that it was just coders having fun with non-coders, but now I know that there is a clear distinction. article is an independent item that can be used constantly. section, on the other hand, is a type of organizing tool used to separate paragraphs. As for the “How a screen reader user accesses the web” reading, it was an eye opener. Although as designers, user-friendly designs are always a priority, it was a “cool idea” that eventually vanished due to time constraints and dead ends. Another factor that stood out in the reading, is that although the creators had user accessibility in mind, it became an inconvenience to the user when they were not able to skip certain parts of the website. In order to avoid such episodes in the near future, coders should allow the target users to “playtest” the prototype before it becomes live to other users.

Reflection on Internet Histories:

I find it fascinating how far the internet has come and even more intriguing that the initial idea was never lost over the years. The timeline put the actual iterations, people involved and everything in between in perspective. I wonder what the future holds for those that want to take the internet one step further.
