Development of my website (Part Something):

The development of my website has not moved from the last time we spoke. I’ve been going through my lectures once more and refreshing this ‘ole noggin. This isn’t where I wanted to be in my progress however, this was a necessary step to better understand my website and ways to improve it.

API's: What is it:

API’s is a complicated topic as I haven’t recovered from javascript (teehee). On a more serious note, API’s stand for Application Programming Interface, this means that it allows two applications to “talk” to each other. This sounds complicated however, we use API’s on a daily basis without realising it. For example, when playing a mobile game and a message appears on your device, you can interact with both programmes at the same time. The technical challenges I foresee is how to keep the audience’s attention while another program runs in the background. I will have to design my website in a way that does not lose my user. This will be a challenge and will be time consuming since this may take the designer back to wireframes and planning.

Reflection on the reading:

I was fortunate enough to have a semester course in gender studies a few years ago. I feel that a class like that is important for future designers and developers as it broadens your perspective and creates a better understanding of todays world. This class allowed me to understand the LGBTQ community better and the daily struggles they go through. If something as current and new as a body scanner cannot cater for people, what is happening? Costanza-Chock (2020) highlights the difficulties faced as a person part of the LGBTQ community. As a result of this technological discrimination, people don’t feel safe. The main question posed in the reading is “how can designers help?”. This is a challenge that forces designers to be more knowledgeable and aware of such situations. For an issue like this to be addressed, designers must ensure and constantly better the “digital justice principles” (Costanza-Chock: 2020) so that it is engraved in societies mind.

Reference:

Costanza-Chock, S. 2020. Design Justice: Community-Led Practices to Build the World's We Need.
