Process Mashup Lecture Slides:

The lecture slides became an aid to developing my website, especially for the design section of my website. The goal alignment for my website is that I would like my users to explore and have fun interacting with my design without any complications. My website is simple and functional which means that the user will not get lost or frustrated. The most interesting parts in my website are the title pages for each section. The content mapping on the other hand was easy on paper but a challenge in code. I managed to further simplify the design in order to make the design breathable. The interface elements are easy to spot and use. The user can navigate their next move easily with scratching for additional buttons. Overall, the slides benefitted me immensely .

Reflection on the readings:

User experience ties in with human computer interaction, and as technology progresses so does the emphasis for interaction and usefulness in design. Carrol and Thomas in Hassenzal and Tractinsky (2011) state that design is not seen as fun, but once people realise many open with a fun mindset or approach to design, the possibilities become endless. I agree with this approach, if fun can influence user experience, then wouldn't that mean that the design was successful? A key aspect to make a design successful is to have behavioural goals that create a sort of intimacy between user and computer. It is a challenging task to accomplish however through the use of a focus group, designers can pin point elements and emotions to create a better user experience.

Reference:

Hassenzal, M and Tractinsky, N. 2011. User Experience: a research agenda.
