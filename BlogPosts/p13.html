Reflection on the readings:

Kasapis (2020) states that during the pandemic, the digital inequalities surfaced as the world was forced to go digital. Not many people had access to a digital device or the internet for that matter. This brought about a disadvantage to a large number of people. Daniolou (2020), elaborates that without internet access, there was a barrier for “equal opportunity and cultural growth”. Rural areas struggled the most as they were shut out of the digital world. Such places have a massive disadvantage due to the lack of education, “lower income and restricted healthcare and travel services” (Daniolou:2020). Once the government took action, there was a positive outcome that led people to develop and encourage connectivity. Internet connectivity should be seen as a basic need as it plays a big role in our lives, from learning, teaching and even for connecting with different people. There are downsides to the internet such as illegal activities but that cannot stop the world from growing. In order to decrease such activities, there should be “proper education” (Daniolou:2020) put in place.

Reference:

Kasapis, S. 2020. Internet Access is a Fundamental Right. Daniolou, C. 2020. The Need for Global Internet Connectivity.
