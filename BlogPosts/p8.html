Although I submitted my assignment late due to my code “acting up”, I felt that it was something to be proud of as I initially saw coding as intimidating. The biggest struggle was the media queries of some of my pages as well as the layout warping during my last-minute tweaking. This was disheartening since I could not find the fault in time. How do this weeks readings impact South African Web Devs:

As a South African, I can say that Africa has come a long way in technological advances however, not long enough to go head-to-head with international developers. I feel that South Africa has the potential to stand out, however guidance and resources do not permit such ventures. Another issue that South Africa faces is that “cheaper is better” and “safe designs” overtake the will to be unique. This limits the skill sets of companies and developers. Western influence plays a big part in limiting our potential as it affects participation and representation. The world follows Western influence however the western side makes their own rules and breaks them once in a while, creatively of course. According to Graham et al (2015), African is stuck in the “technical apartheid” as we have not moved. The digital world waits for no-one and the sooner South Africa realizes that, the better it would be for local developers. In order for us to catch this digital train to the future, we need to apply and “equalize the opportunity of knowledge and culture” (Graham et al: 2017) the South African way.

Reference:

Graham, M, Sabbata, S, Zook, M. 2015. Geography and Environment: Towards the study of information geophraphies.
