Reflection of readings:
I noticed that the readings were futuristic in terms of mass production, technological improvements as well as how information can either help or destroy the future generation. “As we may think” by Vannevar Bush (1945) was most enjoyable to read as his views of the world and examples mentioned created a better understanding about where the world was and will be heading towards. “The Library of Babel” by Borges (1944) approached the same idea as Bush but in a more fictional angle. I preferred the realist approach by Bush as the text was easier to comprehend whilst in Borges' reading felt like an overload of information. This made me feel claustrophobic as if there was not enough time to go through all the books in the library that the writer described. Overall, the readings created awareness of the past and future possibilities of accessing information and how it has evolved in order to better lives. One of the factors mentioned in both readings that I find rather humorous, is that the futuristic vision that both writers had, became the reality that they believed in. This is evident in today's technological advances such as the internet and smart devices used to hold information. It was as if they predicted how information would one day be stored and accessed. It was a rather fascinating point of view to read which makes me wonder how information will be stored in the future years to come.
