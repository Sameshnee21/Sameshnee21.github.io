Reflection on why code matters:

When thinking about how our code matters in digital colonialisms, can you identify any things in your own coding practice that you should be aware of: Based on the reading, “How we code matters” (2022), is a way of expressing oneself through language, that being coding. It is a technical language that needs to be carefully thought through and applied for others to understand. As people we are conditioned to think and act in a certain way due to colonialism. We tend to follow the western ways as they look and feel “right”. The digital knowledge that we have now was underlined by “techno-colonialism” (Geyser: 2022) which in turn altered digital knowledge. This means that there is much more out there than we’ve known and been exposed to. The digital world is growing rapidly and the software sector is guilty of “exclusion, marginalization, unethical technologies and research practices” (Geyser: 2022). We can solve this problem by being more involved in the technological war created by the past. This is not an easy fix, but it will take years in order to reach the heights or even surpass the western world. Coding is becoming a universal language of the world. It is the type of language that can put South African on the map.

Reference:

Geyser, H. 2022. How We Code Matters: Introducing Critical Code Literacies.
