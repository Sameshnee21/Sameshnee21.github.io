INTRODUCTION

When a person gains access into a system, they initially engage the controls within that system. This experience is defined as the user interface or (UI). The user interface (UI), “is an interface design for machines and software, such as computers, mobile phones, and other electronic devices” (Nurpalah et al., 2021). Functionally, the user interface provides a link or communication on the main operating system to ensure that the user can fully experience the full scope of the system being accessed (Nurpalah et al., 2021). In contrast, user experience or (UX), “is about how a person feels” when that engagement between themselves and the system is realized (Nurpalah et al., 2021). Nurpalah et al (2021), affirms that a system “can be a website, software, or other computer application and program” within the prevailing age where human and machine engagements are a common narrative. UI and UX, is therefore a manifestation of the system or platform, that is designed to appeal to the user and draw their attention to the contents of that system. The interaction with users is not a condition of all systems. For example, ubiquitous systems exist where technology facilitates certain activities to be accomplished without the specific interaction between the system and the user (Resnick, 2013). Ubiquitous technology provides new challenges for functional design since user experience is limited or absent. These systems therefore have a specific design and incorporates limited interaction between the user and the system as part of its design (Resnick, 2013). In the modern era however, increasing technology, induce users to seek more interaction. According to Ballatore et al., (2017), “our spatial interactions and experiences with the world are increasingly digital”. If we look at the World Wide Web for example, user interaction is reaching 3.5 billion people who have the capacity irrespective of location to access and contribute to creating knowledge (Bloom, 2004).

INTERACTION STRUCTURED BY UI AND UX

A well-constructed user interface is central to a valuable user experience. If the user interface is difficult to use, chances are that the user will lose interest in the product or service being sold or alternatively contribute to increased maintenance costs (The Basic Principles of User Interface Design, n.d.). According to the ISO 9241 report, 3 usability is defined as: “the extent to which a product can be used by specified users to achieve specified goals with effectiveness, efficiency, and satisfaction in a specified context of use” (Alomari et al., 2020). The augmentation of the digital world through technology means an expansion of the user experience. Digitization and content creation does not only replicate the world in another form but also recreates it (Alomari et al., 2020). If we look at search engines for example, Ballatore et al., (2017) claims that they have formed a knowledge architecture with a specific set of principles, lucidities and preferences. UX is a result of a “user’s internal state, characteristics of the designed system and the context within which the interaction occurs” (Hassenzahl & Tractinsky, 2006). The culmination of this, opens a number of creative and innovative prospects especially for user experience(Hassenzahl & Tractinsky, 2006). This however does not exist in isolation as any user experience emanates from interaction(Cybulski & Horbinski, 2020). According to Cybulski & Horbinski, (2020), user interface are the mechanisms that make communication possible. With the level and complexity of interaction ethical considerations become more prominent. According to Gray & Chivukula, (2019), there has not been enough research that outlines the ethical parameters within which designers are comfortable.

ETHICS OF INTERACTION

Over the years, there has been keen interest on ethics and its impact on design and technology (Findeli, 2001; Gray & Chivukula, 2019). A research project “Prometheus Enlightened” conducted on design ethics and the responsibilities of the designer during 1989 -1992 concluded the following i.e., the definition of professional responsibility requires clarity on the aim of design, importance should be given to development of “design education” and lastly designers should be accountable for their work. With the evolution of design from the Bauhaus line, the design landscape has changed to accommodate the effects of environmental, social and cultural issues to formulate a more balanced perspective. The design and development of art may no longer be seen as a normal course of events without considering ethics as the framework since designers are involved in the act of recreating their version of the biosphere (Findeli, 2001). 4 Increasing technological advancements and digitization means that designers play a significant role in all aspects of everyday life. With the explosion of the World wide Web and information sourcing to understand the population better, digital discrimination is evident from the huge disparities that exist from a social, economic and cultural perspective. The digital divide widens further when we view participation, access to technology between developed countries and developing countries. Digital disparities such as those linked to skills, access and ability affect a significant section of the world. Further augmentation of the internet in everyday life allows for these disparities to leach into areas such as race, class, gender and other aspects of cultural segmentation (Robinson et al., 2015).

ANALYSIS OF UI AND UX OF THE WEBSITE SOLAR ADVICE

The website chosen for analysis is “Solar Advice”. “Solar Advice” is a company providing solar solutions to households and corporate enterprises (SolarAdvice, 2023). Touted by “MY Broadband” as one of the top home solar companies in the country, they provide a range of solutions for the massive power outages experienced by the country. In general, their website is easy to use and has a range of solutions to view. The general design of the website is a 2D layout, however it is smooth and transitions into the next page easily through the user interface. The user interface tools are clearly marked and positioned at the top of the screen so that minimal effort is used in trying to locate a particular page. Scrolling through the home page provides a range of information and guidelines. The 2D UI layout has two advantages in that it is follows a traditional format arrangement as found in web and mobile design. The UI components are crafted on traditional usage norms with functions available by a quick scan. Although set within a 3D space, it brings forth some disadvantages such as it cannot fully exploit the 3D space and use technology such as virtual reality. In addition, with the 2D layout the “visual impact on the user experience is much lower than expected” (Xing et al., n.d.). Websites provide a much more interactive experience for accomplishing activities(Aizpurua et al., 2016). The accomplishment of a website may not only depend on its actual features, but also on how it is experienced by users (Hassenzahl, 2009). The user experience provided by the website “SolarAdvice” is good since it 5 has a number of features that makes it interesting for the user. The range of solutions and the ability to tailor each solution to fit the user’s budget also makes it advantageous. The website provides a dynamic experience since it is easily accessible and useable (Aizpurua et al., 2016). The website is also aesthetically pleasing to the eye and users can easily experience the seamless transition to the graphical interface. According to Lanquetin, (2006), ethical principles such as computer as information repository, information ownership, responsibilities, computers as thinking machines and communication in networks motivated by Parker et al., (1990) and Johnson, (1993) have to be considered. The “SolarAdvice” website acts as a repository for personal information when a quotation is requested, and payment is done. This information can be stolen as the information is stored. The site also does not provide any information about the company and its employees; therefore, the ownership of the information can be disputed. The website details are not clearly expressed, so it is not clearly stated who is responsible for the content or the content is stated correctly. In addition, computers “are thinking machines”(Lanquetin, 2006), and information can be compromised. Finally, communication in networks can be compromised to pose as employees and therefore facilitate fraudulent sales.

CONCLUSION

In this paper we have described how the “SolarAdvice” website has structured UI and UX for improved interaction. Based on the user interface provided by the tools on the website, the general construction, usability and seamless transition from one page to the next combined with the range of information to prospective buyers made the overall user experience a pleasant one. Although these aspects contribute to a positive experience and has significant chance of increasing sales, ethical considerations of the website has to be noted. “SolarAdvice” has to review the aspects that may compromise the website such as information security and the threat of the system being hacked and strengthen these to ensure an ethical system.
